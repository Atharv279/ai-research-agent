### Summary

**Summary:**

EcoServe is an LLM serving system designed for commodity GPU clusters without high-performance interconnects. It implements a partially disaggregated (PaDG) strategy to reduce cross-instance interference and improve performance. The project provides a macro instance implementation, which realizes the PaDG strategy on top of vLLM.

**Key innovations:**

EcoServe's key innovation lies in its partially disaggregated (PaDG) strategy, which mitigates prefill–decode interference by prefilling and decoding within instances along the time dimension and activating cooperating groups across instances to form macro instances. This approach improves goodput and reduces TTFT (Time-To-First-Transfer). The adaptive scheduler and mitosis scaling mechanism further enhance performance.

**Tech stack:**

The primary language used in EcoServe is Python, with CUDA being a secondary dependency. The tech stack also includes C++, C, CMake, Shell, Dockerfile, setuptools, and pyproject.

**Potential impact:**

EcoServe has the potential to significantly impact the AI/ML ecosystem by providing an efficient LLM serving system for commodity GPU clusters. This could make high-performance computing more accessible and cost-effective, enabling researchers and developers to run complex AI models on smaller-scale infrastructure. The PaDG strategy and adaptive scheduling mechanism used in EcoServe could also be applied to other use cases beyond LLM serving, potentially leading to further innovations in the field of AI/ML.
