It takes 24 kHz mono speech waveforms as input, splits them into patches, and uses a causal convolution-transformer autoencoder to reduce the frame rate from 100 Hz to 12.5 Hz. The model then quantizes the latent representation into semantic and acoustic dimensions and trains it using feature matching, ASR distillation, L1 reconstruction, STFT magnitude reconstruction, and VQ commitment loss.

The Voxtral Codec's ability to achieve such low bitrates while maintaining reasonable quality is notable. Additionally, the model's design, which includes multiple encoder stages, a causal convolution-transformer autoencoder, and whisper-based ASR distillation support, demonstrates innovative approaches to improving audio compression.
